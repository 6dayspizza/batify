what should I do if I detect broken bones?

if there are any broken bones involved, call the vet, no matter what day or time.

I can't properly examine the bat because it's too flustered.

store it in one of the boxes with the usual supplies. add some of the buffalo worms, in case it gets hungry. stick a note to the box that you were not able to finish the examination. important: if you can spot some blood and/or broken bones, don't just leave it in the box, but also call the vet.

that bat looks somewhat (or very) sticky.

there is an emergency-cleanup-kit in the cupboard. use the linseed oil to gently rub the stickiness out of its fur. then, use the baby shampoo to remove the oil. repeat process, until bat returns to its usual fluffy self.

I have no idea what sex this is.

just continue the registration without filling that part in. fortunately, for bats it doesn't really matter for the care instructions if it's a boy or a girl. :]

there are barely any mealworms left.

there is a conversion table for mealworms to buffalos, and back. you can also consider the automatic conversion rate given when you fill in the form.

I feel a bit bad for the worms...

as of yet, we don't know everything about mealworms that is there to know. it is said that they have a "very simple" nervous system. it is believed to be so simple, that you can cut them apart, and the individual parts will still continue to function. that is why we often can still see them move, although they are beheaded. there is no definite answer to whether they perceive pain the same way we do. for this reason, we aim to kill them as quickly as possible. that they have some sort of awareness as to what's about to happen to them, is highly doubtable.

is there any vegetarian alternative we could feed the bats?

no, unfortunately there isn't. out there, our local bats have a very exclusive diet. it's insects. they eat between 1500 and 2500 specimens per night. as it is not possible for us to feed them that amount of insects per day in our station, we give them mealworms instead. this diet has proven to be successful.
